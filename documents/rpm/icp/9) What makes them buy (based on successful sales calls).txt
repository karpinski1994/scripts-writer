3. Buying Triggers & Emotional Hooks
Trigger 1: The "Middleman" Realization
* Motivation: Anger/Unfairness.
* The Hook: "You are doing 100% of the work, but your agency is taking 60% of the check."
* Evidence: John and Jose both realized their US clients were paying $120k+, but they only saw $60k.
Trigger 2: Imposter Syndrome (The Seniority Gap)
* Motivation: Status/Security.
* The Hook: "You have 15 years of experience, but you're terrified of a LeetCode algorithm test. Let's fix your foundations."
* Evidence: Carlos and Bastian were both "scared" of technical interviews despite being veterans.
Trigger 3: Time with Family / Freedom
* Motivation: Freedom/Love.
* The Hook: "Earn in 20 hours what you currently earn in 60. Get your life back."
* Evidence: John and Jose specifically mentioned wanting to spend "quality time" with their children and wives.
Trigger 4: The AI Urgency
* Motivation: Fear of Obsolescence.
* The Hook: "AI isn't taking your job, but a programmer who knows how to use AI will. Get on the train or get left at the station."
* Evidence: John and Carlos both mentioned learning AI because "it’s time to join the train."

5. Tactical Advice for Ad Creatives
1. Stop using "Corporate Speak": Use "middleman tax," "paycheck to paycheck," and "LeetCode anxiety."
2. The "20 Years vs. Junior" Paradox: Target senior devs who feel they’ve forgotten the basics. "15 years of experience but still sweating when the screen share starts? We need to talk."
3. The "Agency Robbery" Angle: "Your US client thinks they're paying you $80/hr. You're only seeing $30. Where is the rest going?"
4. The Lifestyle Close: "Relocate to Spain or Thailand on a US salary without needing a visa." (Directly from Aleksander and Suman’s desires).
